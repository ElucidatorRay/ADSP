ADSP: Advanced Dataset for Shadow Processing, enabling visible occluders via synthesizing strategy.

Chang-Yu Hsieh, Jian-Jiun Ding
National Taiwan University
ACCV 2024
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

We noticed that the existing datasets for shadow removal have multiple limitations, which reduce both the generalization capability and the robustness of model. The proposed novel synthesizing strategy can produce shadow-free image with visible occluders and high quality.

Abstract

Shadows can lead to malfunctions in computer vision, making shadow removal an essential task for restoring underlying information. For a long time, researchers have proposed hand-crafted methods based on observing shadow formation models. Then, deep-learning-based solutions have further advanced performance in restoration quality. However, existing datasets have several limitations, such as lacking occluders, restricted camera angles, and inconsistency. In this paper, a novel benchmark called the Advanced Dataset for Shadow Processing (ADSP) is introduced. Through the synthesizing strategy, the ADSP becomes the first dataset containing outdoor images with occluders. Statistical analysis and experiments demonstrate that the ADSP has the advantages of lower domain shifting, matching real-world scenarios, and sufficient generalizing capability. Moreover, as a reference for the removal task, we also propose the Segmented Refinement Removal Network (SRRN), which includes three subnets for shadow removal, color adjustment, and boundary smoothing, respectively. It achieves state-of-the-art performance and can be set as a reference for shadow removal.

Characteristics of the proposed ADSP

The proposed synthesizing strategy can generate outdoor shadow pair with visible occluders.

Because of no restriction of occluder, the degree of freedom of cameras depression angle can be higher.

The inconsistency problem among non-shadow region were mitigated as much as possible, which is helpful for reducing bias of both training and evaluation.

Our process has potential to provide diverse data. Theoretically, shadow cast by any movable object is collectable.

Evaluation result

Statistical analysis proves that our ADSP shows larger challenges than existing dataset, because of the darker shadows. Moreover, the data pairs collected by synthesizing strategy achieve great supression on inconsistency problems on non-shadow region.

Domain shift experiments on two state-of-the-art models and four dataset verify that the proposed ADSP has great generalization capability.

Baseline model

We propose a three staged Segmented Refinement Removal Network (SRRN) to set a baseline on ADSP for shadow removal.

According to comparisons with multiple state-of-the-art models, the SRRN achieve competitive performance.
